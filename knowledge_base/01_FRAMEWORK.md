# Dr. Vijay Malik's Forensic Accounting Framework
## "Peaceful Investing" Methodology

---

## Overview

Dr. Vijay Malik's approach analyzes companies across **5 pillars** using 10+ years of data from annual reports, credit rating reports, and regulatory filings. The framework is designed to identify both investment-worthy companies and potential frauds/value traps.

---

## PILLAR 1: FINANCIAL & BUSINESS ANALYSIS

### 1.1 Sales Growth
- Track 10-year sales growth (YoY and CAGR)
- Growth >30-35% is "highly unstable" and usually driven by external factors
- Compare standalone vs consolidated figures

### 1.2 Profitability Analysis
| Metric | What to Check | Red Flag | Green Flag |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| **OPM (Operating Profit Margin)** | Stability over 10 years | Wildly fluctuating (e.g., 3% to 21%) | Stable or improving (e.g., 14-16%) |
| **NPM (Net Profit Margin)** | Trend and correlation with OPM | Declining despite stable OPM | Improving alongside OPM |
| **Raw Material Cost % of Sales** | Pricing power indicator | Fluctuating 53-77% | Stable or declining |
| **Tax Payout Ratio** | Compare to standard 33-34% | Significantly below without clear reason | At or near standard rate |

### 1.3 Cash Flow Analysis (THE CORE TEST)
- **Cumulative PAT (cPAT) vs Cumulative CFO (cCFO)** over 10 years
  - If cCFO >= cPAT: Profits are converting to cash (GOOD)
  - If cCFO < cPAT: Money is getting stuck in working capital (BAD)
- **Free Cash Flow (FCF)** = CFO - Capex
  - Positive FCF = company can self-fund growth
  - Negative FCF = dependent on external capital
- Check if dividends are paid from FCF or from borrowed money

### 1.4 Leverage Analysis
| Metric | Concern Level |
|--------|--------------|
| Debt to Equity Ratio | Rising over years = danger |
| Interest Coverage | Declining = stress |
| Debt trend over 10 years | Continuously increasing = structural problem |

## PILLAR 2: OPERATING EFFICIENCY ANALYSIS

### The Three Key Ratios

#### 2.1 Net Fixed Asset Turnover (NFAT)
- **Formula:** Sales / Net Fixed Assets
- **Interpretation:**
  - NFAT > 5: Very asset-light BUT low barriers to entry, commodity business, no pricing power
  - NFAT 2-5: Sweet spot -- moderate capital needs, some barriers
  - NFAT < 1.5: Capital-intensive, needs continuous debt for growth
  - NFAT < 1: Each Rs 1 of additional sales needs > Rs 1 of new assets (danger zone)
- **Watch for:** Declining NFAT over time = new projects have lower efficiency

#### 2.2 Inventory Turnover Ratio (ITR)
- **Formula:** Sales / Average Inventory
- **Watch for:** Declining ITR = funds getting stuck in inventory
- **Industry context:** Software companies have ITR 40-100; manufacturers typically 4-12

#### 2.3 Receivables Days
- **Formula:** (Trade Receivables / Sales) x 365
- **Watch for:**
  - Increasing receivables days = declining bargaining power
  - Receivables > 6 months old = high risk of write-off
  - Compare stated credit period vs actual receivables days
- **Best case:** Near-zero (cash & carry models like GM Breweries, Sreeleathers)

## PILLAR 3: MARGIN OF SAFETY IN BUSINESS

### 3.1 Self-Sustainable Growth Rate (SSGR)
- **Concept:** The maximum rate at which a company can grow using ONLY internally generated funds
- **Formula:** SSGR = NFAT x NPM x (1 - Dividend Payout Ratio) - Depreciation Rate
- **Interpretation:**
  - SSGR > Actual Growth: Company can self-fund (EXCELLENT)
  - SSGR = Actual Growth: Balanced (GOOD)
  - SSGR < Actual Growth: Must raise external capital via debt/equity (RISKY)
  - Negative SSGR: Business is destroying capital (DANGER)
- **Limitation:** Does not account for working capital changes -- must cross-check with cCFO vs cPAT

### 3.2 Free Cash Flow Analysis
- **Net FCF** = FCF + Non-operating income - Interest expense
- Companies with positive FCF don't need debt or equity dilution
- "Playing arbitrage between sources of funds is a treasury operation that should be avoided by non-financial companies"

---

## PILLAR 4: MARGIN OF SAFETY IN MARKET PRICE

### 4.1 P/E Ratio Assessment (Benjamin Graham Framework)
- **Earnings Yield** = 1/PE
- Earnings Yield should exceed long-term G-Sec rate (7-10%)
- At 9-10% G-Sec rates: P/E < 10-11 offers margin of safety
- High P/E can be justified ONLY if SSGR is high AND FCF is positive

### 4.2 Value Trap Warning
- Low P/E alone does NOT mean cheap
- If business has no growth visibility, low P/E may be permanent
- Examples: Chaman Lal Setia, Noida Toll Bridge

### 4.3 Other Valuation Metrics
- P/B Ratio (Price to Book)
- Dividend Yield
- Market Cap Increase vs Retained Earnings over 10 years (value creation check)

## PILLAR 5: QUALITATIVE / MANAGEMENT ANALYSIS

### 5.1 Promoter Behaviour
| Check | Red Flag | Green Flag |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Promoter salary | >10% of PAT; equal salaries for all family members | <2-4% of PAT; performance-linked |
| Promoter shareholding | Declining without valid reason | Stable or increasing via market purchases |
| Share pledging | Any significant pledging | Zero pledging |
| Warrants/preferential allotment | At deep discount to market price | Not used for promoter enrichment |
| Related party transactions | Loans/purchases at non-market rates | Minimal, at arm's length |
| Corporate guarantees | For non-subsidiary group companies | Only for subsidiaries if any |
| Succession planning | No plan visible | Next generation being groomed |

### 5.2 Corporate Governance
- Read annual reports for 10 years ("they unfold like a storybook")
- Check for errors/inconsistencies in annual reports
- Verify independence of "independent" directors
- Check for credit rating shopping (switching agencies after downgrades)
- Review auditor observations -- repeated issues = management ignoring concerns
- CSR compliance

### 5.3 Key Principle
> "Focus on disclosure standards, not amounts. Even small undisclosed items reveal management attitude toward transparency."

## KEY FORMULAS SUMMARY

```
SSGR = NFAT x NPM x (1 - DPR) - Depreciation Rate
FCF = CFO - Capex
Net FCF = FCF + Non-operating Income - Interest Expense
Earnings Yield = 1 / PE Ratio
NFAT = Sales / Net Fixed Assets
ITR = Sales / Average Inventory
Receivables Days = (Trade Receivables / Sales) x 365
Value Creation = (Market Cap Increase over 10 yrs) / (Retained Earnings over 10 yrs)
PBT/NFA Ratio = Profit Before Tax / Net Fixed Assets (compare to bank FD rate)
```
