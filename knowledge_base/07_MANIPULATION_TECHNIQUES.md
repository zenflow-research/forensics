# Financial Manipulation Techniques Encyclopedia
## Documented Techniques from Dr. Vijay Malik's Analysis with Real Examples

---

## 1. REVENUE & PROFIT MANIPULATION

### 1.1 Aggressive Revenue Recognition
- **Technique:** Recognizing revenue at factory dispatch instead of buyer acceptance
- **Impact:** Inflates revenue and profits before sale is truly confirmed
- **Example:** Indo Count Industries (export company recognizing at dispatch, not delivery)
- **How to Detect:** Read revenue recognition policy in Notes to Accounts; compare to industry peers

### 1.2 Percentage of Completion Method (POCM) Abuse
- **Technique:** Recognizing revenue based on costs incurred, not cash collected
- **Impact:** Creates unbilled revenue, masks disputes, inflates profitability
- **Example:** KNR Constructions (Rs 80-85 cr unbilled), MBL Infrastructure
- **How to Detect:** Check unbilled revenue balance; check "Due to customers" liability; track % of revenue that is actually collected in cash

### 1.3 Capitalizing Revenue Expenses
- **Technique:** Showing advertising, brand development, or other operating costs as intangible assets
- **Impact:** Removes costs from P&L, artificially inflates profits over multiple years
- **Example:** Globus Spirits (Rs 36 cr advertising as intangible assets)
- **How to Detect:** Check intangible asset additions; compare to peer accounting treatment

### 1.4 Asset Revaluation to Inflate Profits
- **Technique:** Revaluing depreciating assets (DG sets, plant, buildings) UPWARD, creating revaluation reserve, then reducing depreciation charge
- **Impact:** Lower depreciation = higher profits for years
- **Example:** Indo Count Industries (Rs 178.7 cr inflated PBT: Rs 84 cr realized, Rs 94 cr pending)
- **How to Detect:** Check revaluation reserves; compare asset values to original cost; verify depreciation rates

### 1.5 Reserve Transfer to Nullify Amortization
- **Technique:** Transferring amounts from General Reserve to P&L Statement to offset goodwill amortization
- **Impact:** Goodwill amortization expense effectively disappears from P&L
- **Example:** Emami Limited
- **How to Detect:** Track reserve movements; check if goodwill amortization is fully hitting P&L

### 1.6 Business Reconstruction Reserve (BRR)
- **Technique:** Court-approved adjustment of massive write-offs directly to balance sheet reserves, bypassing P&L entirely
- **Impact:** Billions in losses never appear in the income statement
- **Example:** Escorts (Rs 1,000+ cr of USA subsidiary write-offs)
- **How to Detect:** Check for any scheme of arrangement; read court-approved restructuring documents; compare equity movements to P&L

## 2. CASH FLOW MANIPULATION

### 2.1 Loan Proceeds as Operating Cash Flow
- **Technique:** Classifying new loan proceeds as part of CFO instead of Cash Flow from Financing (CFF)
- **Impact:** Inflates operating cash flow, hides debt dependency
- **Example:** Sreeleathers (Rs 11.5 cr loan shown as CFO inflow)
- **How to Detect:** Reconcile every item in CFO with balance sheet movements; check if "other current liabilities" in CFO includes new borrowings

### 2.2 Asset Sale Proceeds as Operating Cash Flow
- **Technique:** Showing one-time asset sale proceeds under CFO instead of Cash Flow from Investing (CFI)
- **Impact:** Inflates recurring operating cash generation
- **Example:** PIX Transmissions (Rs 134 cr from hose division sale shown under CFO)
- **How to Detect:** Check CFI for absence of large disposal proceeds; cross-reference with press releases about asset sales

### 2.3 Interest Misclassification
- **Technique:** Showing interest expense under CFO instead of CFF
- **Impact:** Understates CFF (making financing look healthier), overstates CFO
- **Example:** Supreme Industries (until FY2012), Skipper
- **How to Detect:** Check where interest paid appears; verify consistency across years

### 2.4 CFO Calculation Errors (Intentional or Not)
- **Technique:** Mathematical errors in the cash flow statement
- **Impact:** Overstates cash generation
- **Example:** HEG (Rs 55.5 cr overstatement in FY2013)
- **How to Detect:** Manually recalculate CFO from the components provided; reconcile with balance sheet

### 2.5 Buyback Under Wrong Section
- **Technique:** Showing share buyback spending under CFI instead of CFF
- **Impact:** Distorts both investing and financing cash flow sections
- **Example:** Supreme Industries (FY2009)
- **How to Detect:** Check buyback announcements against CFF entries

## 3. BALANCE SHEET MANIPULATION

### 3.1 Forex Losses Capitalized in Assets
- **Technique:** Adding foreign exchange losses to CWIP or fixed asset costs instead of expensing through P&L
- **Impact:** Hides forex losses from profit calculation; inflates asset base
- **Example:** HEG (Rs 192 cr hidden in fixed assets)
- **How to Detect:** Check CWIP additions against project cost details; look for forex-related additions to fixed assets in notes

### 3.2 Depreciation Method Changes
- **Technique:** Switching between SLM (Straight Line) and WDV (Written Down Value) methods
- **Impact:** Alters depreciation charge, net fixed asset values, NFAT ratios, and profitability
- **Example:** Bodal Chemicals (changed SLM to WDV in FY2014, back to SLM in FY2018; WDV change reduced fixed assets by 35%)
- **How to Detect:** Read accounting policy section for depreciation method; check for changes; understand impact on all ratios

### 3.3 Unrealized Losses Excluded from P&L
- **Technique:** Not including mark-to-market losses on derivative contracts in the income statement
- **Impact:** Overstates profits for the period
- **Example:** AIA Engineering (Rs 25 cr in FY2010, Rs 14.3 cr in FY2011, Rs 8.3 cr in FY2012 -- auditor flagged each year)
- **How to Detect:** Read auditor's emphasis of matter; check derivative disclosures vs P&L entries

### 3.4 IndAS Transition Manipulation
- **Technique:** Using the transition from old GAAP to Ind-AS to revalue assets, change classifications, inflate equity
- **Impact:** Historical ratio comparisons become unreliable; equity inflated
- **Example:** Finolex Industries (equity inflated by Rs 1,000+ cr from marking Finolex Cables investment to market value)
- **How to Detect:** Read the Ind-AS transition notes carefully; calculate impact on all key ratios

## 4. PROMOTER ENRICHMENT SCHEMES

### 4.1 Warrant Arbitrage
- **Technique:** Getting warrants issued at a price significantly below market, converting when profitable, letting them lapse when not
- **Impact:** "75% beneficial to promoters, 25% to company" -- promoters get a free call option
- **Examples:** Rexnord (43% discount), Indo Count (Rs 4.97 cr invested, shares worth Rs 86 cr), Granules India (Rs 43.4 cr gain)
- **How to Detect:** Compare warrant exercise price to prevailing market price; check if promoters sell shares shortly after conversion

### 4.2 Interest Rate Arbitrage (The Vicious Cycle)
- **Technique:** Promoters draw maximum salary -> company faces cash shortage -> promoters "lend" money back at above-market interest rates
- **Impact:** Promoters earn both salary AND interest income at company's expense
- **Examples:** PIX Transmissions (12%), Bharat Rasayan (10% vs 6% FD rate), KNR (10.3%)
- **How to Detect:** Compare promoter loan interest rates to company's bank borrowing rate and FD rate

### 4.3 Circular Shareholding
- **Technique:** Company invests in promoter's private entity -> that entity uses funds to buy shares of the listed company -> promoter stake increases using minority shareholders' money
- **Impact:** Promoter control increases without them spending their own money
- **Examples:** Sreeleathers/Shoeline Trading, Dynemic Products/DHPL, Escorts/ESCOTRAC/EFILL
- **How to Detect:** Trace company investments to private entities; check if those entities hold shares in the company

### 4.4 Asset Stripping via Related Parties
- **Technique:** Selling valuable assets to promoter entities at below-fair-value; buying poor assets from promoters at above-fair-value
- **Examples:**
  - Kokuyo Camlin: Pre-school business sold to promoter family at 1/10th of investment
  - Atul: Sold Amal Ltd stake to promoters just as the company was reviving
  - Stovec: Sold sugar screen business to group company, leased back the same assets
  - Globus: Bought promoter company ADL at Rs 45 cr vs Rs 9.91 cr book value
- **How to Detect:** Check valuation reports for asset sales/purchases with related parties; compare to market values

### 4.5 Pre-Event Cash Extraction
- **Technique:** Paying unusually large dividends just before IPO or major share sale
- **Impact:** Promoters extract accumulated cash before new shareholders can benefit
- **Examples:** Quick Heal (large dividend before IPO), IndiGo Airlines (similar pattern)
- **How to Detect:** Check dividend history for unusual spikes before IPO or significant equity events

### 4.6 Promoter Shareholding Inflation
- **Technique:** Using company-controlled entities (trusts, subsidiaries) to hold company shares, reporting these as "promoter shareholding"
- **Impact:** Reported promoter stake far exceeds actual economic ownership
- **Example:** Escorts (reported 40.25% but effective ~17.6%; ESCOTRAC and EFILL funded by Escorts held 19.33%)
- **How to Detect:** Read shareholding pattern footnotes; trace trust/entity ownership back to see if company itself funds them

## 5. SPECULATIVE / NON-CORE MANIPULATION

### 5.1 Derivative Speculation Disguised as Hedging
- **Technique:** Taking speculative derivative positions and calling them "hedges"
- **Impact:** Large, unpredictable gains/losses hit P&L; potential for catastrophic losses
- **Examples:** Emami (naked derivatives on forecasted transactions), Finolex Industries ($20.8M loss), Indo Count (Rs 150 cr loss, led to CDR)
- **How to Detect:** Check if derivative gains/losses appear in P&L (effective hedges go to OCI, not P&L); check auditor commentary on hedge effectiveness

### 5.2 Short-Term Equity Trading by Industrial Companies
- **Technique:** Manufacturing companies investing in 20-30+ listed stocks for short-term trading
- **Impact:** Speculation risk with shareholders' money; management distraction
- **Examples:** Bhageria Industries (Rs 33 cr in 35 stocks), Finolex Cables (20+ stocks)
- **How to Detect:** Check current investment schedule; count number of equity positions

### 5.3 Dividend Stripping
- **Technique:** Buying securities before ex-dividend date, receiving dividend, selling after ex-date at loss
- **Impact:** Creates short-term capital loss to offset other gains while receiving dividend income
- **Example:** Maithan Alloys (Rs 115 cr dividends, Rs 114.6 cr losses)
- **How to Detect:** Check for simultaneous large dividend income and investment losses in same period
