# Key Concepts & Glossary
## Forensic Accounting Terminology from Dr. Vijay Malik

---

## CORE CONCEPTS

### Peaceful Investing
Dr. Malik's proprietary stock analysis approach combining Financial, Business, Management, and Valuation analysis over 10+ years of data. The name reflects the philosophy that thorough analysis leads to "peaceful" investing without anxiety.

### Self-Sustainable Growth Rate (SSGR)
The maximum rate at which a company can grow its sales using only internally generated funds (retained earnings), without needing additional debt or equity. Formula: SSGR = NFAT x NPM x (1-DPR) - Depreciation Rate. Does not factor in working capital changes.

### Free Cash Flow (FCF)
Cash Flow from Operations minus Capital Expenditure. Represents the actual cash available to shareholders after maintaining/growing the business. A company with positive FCF doesn't need external funding.

### Margin of Safety
Benjamin Graham's concept -- the difference by which a stock's value exceeds its price. Dr. Malik applies this at two levels: (1) in business model (SSGR > growth rate, positive FCF), and (2) in purchase price (earnings yield > G-Sec rate, practically P/E < 10-11).

### Value Trap
A stock that appears cheap on P/E ratio but has no growth visibility or deteriorating business quality. The low P/E is not a buying opportunity but a permanent discount reflecting poor prospects.

## FINANCIAL TERMS

### Cumulative PAT (cPAT)
Total Profit After Tax accumulated over multiple years (typically 10). Used in comparison with cCFO.

### Cumulative CFO (cCFO)
Total Cash Flow from Operations accumulated over multiple years. If cCFO >= cPAT, profits are real and converting to cash.

### Net Fixed Asset Turnover (NFAT)
Sales divided by Net Fixed Assets. Measures how efficiently fixed assets generate revenue. Sweet spot: 2-5. Very high (>5) = low barriers to entry. Very low (<1) = capital trap.

### PBT/NFA Ratio
Profit Before Tax divided by Net Fixed Assets. If lower than bank FD rates (6-8%), the business is earning less than a simple bank deposit on its assets.

### Operating Profit Margin (OPM)
Operating Profit / Sales. Stable OPM indicates pricing power. Wildly fluctuating OPM indicates commodity business with no pricing power.

### Earnings Yield
Inverse of P/E ratio (1/PE). Compared to Government Securities rate for margin of safety assessment.

### Dividend Distribution Tax (DDT)
Tax paid by the company on dividends distributed to shareholders (~15%). Abolished from April 2020.

## MANIPULATION-RELATED TERMS

### Credit Rating Shopping
When a company switches credit rating agencies after receiving a downgrade or negative outlook, seeking a more favorable rating from a less stringent agency.

### Trinity Pattern
Simultaneous occurrence of Rising Sales + Rising Receivables + Rising Debt. When disproportionate, often signals aggressive or fictitious accounting.

### Circular Shareholding
A structure where a listed company invests in a promoter's private entity, which then uses those funds to buy shares of the listed company, effectively increasing promoter control using minority shareholders' money.

### Vicious Cycle (Promoter Remuneration)
Pattern where promoters draw maximum salary, creating cash shortfall, then "helpfully" lend money back at above-market interest rates, earning both salary and interest income.

### Dividend Stripping
Buying securities just before ex-dividend date, receiving the dividend, then selling at a loss. Creates capital loss to offset other gains while receiving dividend income.

### Business Reconstruction Reserve (BRR)
Court-approved mechanism to adjust massive write-offs directly to balance sheet reserves, bypassing the P&L statement entirely.

### Percentage of Completion Method (POCM)
Revenue recognition method in construction/EPC where revenue is recognized based on costs incurred (percentage of estimated total cost). Can be manipulated by understating total estimated costs.

### Preferential Allotment of Warrants
Issuing warrants to promoters at a pre-set price, often at a discount to market. Warrants = call options that give promoters risk-free speculation on their own stock.

## GOVERNANCE TERMS

### Related Party Transactions
Transactions between the company and its promoters, their family members, or entities controlled by them. Must be disclosed and ideally at arm's length.

### Corporate Guarantee
A commitment by a company to repay loans taken by another entity if that entity defaults. Red flag when given for non-subsidiary group companies.

### Contingent Liabilities
Potential liabilities that may arise depending on future events (lawsuits, guarantees, disputed taxes). Often where hidden risks lurk.

### CARO (Companies Auditor's Report Order)
Mandatory annexure to auditor's report covering specific matters like statutory dues, related party loans, internal controls. Rich source of red flags.

### Scheme of Arrangement
Court-approved restructuring plan (mergers, demergers, goodwill treatment). Can be used to restructure financials in ways that bypass normal accounting treatment.

### Non-Operating Income
Income from activities outside core business (interest income, forex gains, investment gains). When this forms a large portion of total profits, core business profitability is questionable.

### Naked Derivatives
Derivative positions without an underlying transaction to hedge, creating speculative risk. True hedges should not create P&L impact (they go to OCI).

## BUSINESS TERMS

### Capital-Intensive Business
Business requiring high capex relative to sales. Characterized by low NFAT (<2). Must earn high profitability margins to generate adequate returns, otherwise falls into debt trap.

### Working Capital Intensive
Business where significant funds are tied up in inventory and receivables. Even profitable companies can face liquidity crisis if working capital consumes all profits.

### Contract Manufacturing Paradox
Companies like Amber Enterprises trade profitability for revenue assurance. They accept very low margins (1-3% NPM) in exchange for large order books from big brands. Risk: customers can cancel anytime.

### Operating Leverage
The effect of fixed costs on profit margins. When a company has high fixed costs and low variable costs, small changes in revenue create large changes in profits. Example: Escorts -- every 10,000 tractor change = 1% margin change.

### Export Packing Credit
Short-term working capital facility for exporters at preferential interest rates.

### Letter of Credit (LC)
Banking instrument that helps exporters realize receivables early by having the bank guarantee payment.

---

## REGULATORY TERMS

### CDR (Corporate Debt Restructuring)
A voluntary mechanism where lenders restructure the debt of a stressed borrower. Being in CDR is a severe red flag indicating near-default.

### Anti-Dumping Duty
Import duty imposed when foreign producers sell goods below their home market price or below cost. Companies dependent on this duty face risk when it expires.

### SEZ (Special Economic Zone)
Designated zones with tax benefits for companies setting up operations. Tax holidays from SEZ units are temporary and have expiry dates.

### SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)
Market regulator. SEBI orders against a company or its promoters are serious governance red flags.

### DIN (Director Identification Number)
Unique number assigned to every director. Can be used to trace a director's other company involvements via MCA database.

## DR. MALIK'S KEY PRINCIPLES (QUOTED)

1. > "Read annual reports for at least 10 years -- they unfold like a storybook showing management quality, challenges faced, and decisions made."

2. > "Investors should not take investment decisions based on recent performance -- base decisions on analysis of performance over many years."

3. > "Focus on disclosure standards, not amounts. Even small undisclosed items reveal management attitude toward transparency."

4. > "Any growth more than 30-35% is highly unstable and usually due to external factors beyond company control."

5. > "Assessment of management is partly subjective exercise like choosing friends -- requires judgment beyond numbers."

6. > "One company a year is enough -- an investor does not need to find dozens of good companies."

7. > "A company that generates good FCF post capex would not need any debt or equity dilution."

8. > "Playing arbitrage between sources of funds is a treasury operation that should be avoided by non-financial companies."

9. > "High ROE is not enough in itself -- it combines profitability, asset turnover, AND leverage. Prefer high profitability + high asset turnover + LOW leverage."

10. > "Stock analysis is a never-ending exercise -- define priorities and use checklists to filter efficiently."

11. > "Compare company's claims about macro factors with peer performance to verify truthfulness."

12. > "Credit rating reports provide information not usually available in annual reports (e.g., installed capacity, key risk factors)."
